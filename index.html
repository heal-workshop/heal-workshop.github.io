Overview

As Large Language Models (LLMs) continue to advance, addressing the ''evaluation crisis'' and exploring human-centered evaluation and auditing approaches become increasingly important. Ensuring that these models are effective, ethical, and socially responsible is crucial for their widespread adoption and integration into many real-world applications. We invite authors from all disciplines to submit papers to the CHI 2024 Workshop on Human-centered Evaluation and Auditing of Language Models (HEAL@CHI'24).

This workshop aims to foster discussions on understanding real-world stakeholders' needs and goals, establishing human-centered evaluation and auditing methods for LLMs, and developing tools, processes, and resources to support stakeholders in evaluating and auditing LLMs.

Call for Participation

We welcome participants who work on related areas in supporting human-centered evaluation and auditing of language models. Interested participants will be asked to contribute a short paper to the workshop. Submissions can take several forms:

  1. Empirical understanding of stakeholders' needs and goals of LLM evaluation and auditing
  2. Human-centered evaluation and auditing methods for LLMs
  3. Tools, processes, and guidelines for LLM evaluation and auditing
  4. Discussion of regulatory measures and public policies for LLM auditing

Submissions will go through a jury review process and be selected based on quality of the submission and diversity of perspectives to allow for a meaningful exchange of knowledge between a broad range of stakeholders.
